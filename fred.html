Article 01

Fruits are the mature ovaries of flowering plants, typically containing seeds and are often sweet and fleshy, making them a popular food source. They play a vital role in seed dispersal for plants and offer nutritional benefits to humans.

Types of Fruits: Simple Fruits: Develop from a single ovary of one flower, like apples, peaches, and bananas. Aggregate Fruits: Develop from multiple ovaries of a single flower, like raspberries and strawberries. Multiple Fruits: Develop from the ovaries of multiple flowers that are fused together, like pineapples and figs.

Vegetables are the edible parts of plants, often consumed for their nutritional value. They encompass a wide variety of plant parts, including roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds. Different types of vegetables can be categorized based on their nutrient content and the part of the plant they come from, such as dark green, red and orange, starchy, and beans, peas, and lentils. What are vegetables? In common usage, "vegetable" refers to the edible portions of herbaceous plants that are typically eaten fresh or cooked. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to all edible plant matter.
